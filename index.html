Pomke Nohkan



Hi there! I am a web architect, engineer and development leader with fifteen years commercial experience building scalable web applications using open source software. My speciality is the development of robust systems designed for reliability, security, scalability and maintainability, using modern high-level languages such as JavaScript (Node.js) and Python. I am based in the mountains, east of Melbourne, Australia.

I'm big on functional and asynchronous programming methodologies for building massively scalable services. This is why much of my experience has been with frameworks such as Twisted Python and Node.js. This doesn't mean I'm unfamiliar with OO or threaded programming, I just believe in the right tools for the right job.

I am passionate about diversity and love encouraging others to learn programming. I believe the internet is a tool for liberation that can be given to anyone to empower them to have a voice in the global community.

    I arrived at Small World Social in late 2011, a small online advertising company that had branched out into online training. They had built some custom training modules (video & Flash) on top of a shopping cart system built in Python and Django by contractors, however the system was very difficult to use for the creative staff, was buggy and would crash under load (ORM death), and the contractors had since left.

    I spent 4-6 weeks shoring up and re-factoring the Django implementation, however it was obviously not delivering any benefit for the company and actually hindering development of client content. The owner had a desire to move into more business-focused online training and desired a platform that would scale into global markets.

    I recruited engineers and began work on a new platform, designed specifically around the company's creative workflow. I moved user interactives away from Flash, towards HTML5 technologies in consideration of scalability and futureproofing. A bespoke content management system and deployment front-ends were part of my design to manage client content. By automating much of the manual work, we saw production times for online courses drop from several months to several weeks.

    Fast forward to 2014, Small World Social is now a multinational company which boasts a multi-award winning online learning system, in use by pharmaceutical and tech companies across the world. The platform includes a content management system with tools for clients to self-manage and curate content. Web, html-mobile, native iOS and Google Glass clients integrate with location-based beacon technology to provide training at a user's fingertips in the field. All user activity is tracked and stored to provide information to clients about the performance of their content via a comprehensive analytics system developed using crossfilter.js and D3.js.

    The backend is a SOA-based system of API services doing discrete tasks, capable of being scaled horizontally through load-balanced API endpoints, communicating directly or via message queues. We used MongoDB to provide distributed, scalable data, and Elastic Search for full text indexing. All services were built using Node.js. The front-ends use the one-page-app model and are served as static resources to the browser directly from Amazon S3, rich content such as video and audio media is delivered through CloudFront.

    See Small World Social for more information.
